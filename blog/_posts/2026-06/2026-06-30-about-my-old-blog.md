June 09, 2005, I started writing blog. The reason was I saw a lot of magazine mentioned about blog or somebody said something in their blog, or somebody moved his blog to certain platform. I was wondering what is it. I don't like keep diary, as you know in China we mostly trained to write about certain themes at school, but most topics I was interesting such as gaming, or some memories about how naughty I was, those are not suppose to be written as any writing homework assignments. I don't know how it is in US, but I really hope to write something about what I like. Then I found blog is a great place because it's online, so people generally have no idea who am I(well, until my parents heard about my blog they started to see what I wrote...) 

My previous blog is on a platform called bccn.net (should be translated as Programming China), sounds a big platform but in fact was running by one person. At that time I almost spend my whole day on programming and playing Warcraft, nothing else(hanging out with my that ime girl friends doesn't count as my own time of the day). So that platform fit the themes of my interests. I kept writing and posting anything in my mind, in my heart, interests me, bothers me. My blog ranked the 1st on the platform when they have a ranking system. 

I stopped because the owner of the website was considering changing the blog system into a social media. That time I was in grade 3 of my college in 2006, online social media just become popular, Facebook was a great example. In China, there's a website called xiaonei.com (the title means Within School)start to post advertisement to every school. It start building network between classmates and school mates. I know a lot of my classmates using that to hunting girls to hangout. I hate it and refuse to open my account on it until I graduate from my college. The Xiaonei website began with photo album function, and later it started to have blogging function. People started to write or copy-paste passages on it too. It was a great competitor with the Chinese famous twitter style platform weibo.com. I hate Xiaonei because I like to write my stuff without any people knows who I am in real life(other than the people i hope they knows that was me, mostly the girls I was chasing). The blog system on bccn.net used to be very customizable, it was amazing that the owner can build a website process html and javascript, that's where I learned and practice HTML, maybe he got some webiste template that I don't know. However, later when he decided to change to social media style so the customizable function gone, and more hurts me was I think he changed database, so a lot of long passage I wrote or copy-paste from other webiste(that time there's no Share function on-line, so copy is the only way) are cut in the middle and lost later part of the contents. So that hurt me too much and I stopped posting on it unless there's something I really hope to talk about at somewhere, I will write one or two, but very less often.

That also taught me a lesson, the online platform won't last forever. Even there's a saying that *internet never forget*.  But who knows where to find its memmory. Years later when i saw my classmates uploading picture to Xiaonei, Sina Blog, QQ Space and so exciting to say things like "it's so great that now I don't need to keep the pictures in my harddrive anymore." I knew they are naive. And not suprised me at all, a few years later Tencent's new product WeChat (Weixin in China) came out, most social media got badly impacted because WeChat is not only a chatting software but also have Moments function that combines posting twitter size message with pictures together, and the content automatically shared between friends on one platform. Xiaonei started to getting old, and later it had a few fail maintenances, I heard the owner of Xiaonei was the person like when start working on an idea he's so great and fast, but when he gets tired he will stop doing anything. So Xiaonei platform gone as I expected.

It's wonderful that Github provides the personal webpage function plus it supports Jekyll which is can be a very easy lightweight blog module on my website. How long github will last? I don't know, but at least what I like is, everything I post, I will have at least one local repository just in case something bad happens. Hopefully I am right.

Today, when I added this blog module into my website, I was so surprised that bccn.net is still accessible, and my blog contents mostly still as how they were after that time the big content losts. I'm thinking to move them here. That part of my life, my memories, and my journey to today. 

I will call it Olde Blog with the a faux-archaic spelling of "old" used to evoke a quaint, traditional, or medieval feel. I think that fit what the my previous blog to me.
